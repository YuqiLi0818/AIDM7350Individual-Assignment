Zhanjiang, located in Guangdong Province, China, is a beautiful coastal city known for its rich maritime culture and seafood industry. It has a long history and plays a significant role in China's economic and trade development.

The city boasts stunning natural landscapes, including Donghai Island, one of China's largest islands, and Huguangyan National Geopark, a rare maar lake formed by volcanic activity. Zhanjiang's coastline and beaches attract many tourists seeking relaxation and seafood delicacies.

As a major transportation hub, Zhanjiang has one of China's largest deep-water ports, facilitating international trade. The city is also a leader in agriculture and aquaculture, especially in shrimp farming.
